Boeing Max

Workplace Design

Boeing looked to relocate their 737 MAX design team closer in proximity to the assembly line to accommodate four goals: Increase productivity; increase efficiency; increase communication between teams; and create a direct connection between their engineering staff and the manufacturing of the 737 MAX airplanes.

The solution was to create a “building within a building” – a 120,000 sf, two-level office building inside the WWII-era manufacturing factory.

Facebook Seattle

Workplace Design

Park Place

Lobby Renovation

The updated main entrance and the two new lobby spaces on the first and second floors celebrate the building as a showcase of sustainability and emphasize its owner’s commitment to environmental excellence. The new spaces are an integrated assembly of carefully chosen materials and components. The design represents a timeless solution that gives due heed to the existing building and its structural principles.

Through a series of simple architectural strategies the main entry plaza is given a sense of place and a human scale. A marquee is added on the tall concrete columns which, together with the stainless steel mesh above, ‘captures’ the space, making it feel safe and intimate. A bench successfully activates the plaza and draws people in. The formerly dark main lobby is made luminous and welcoming by updating window glazing, new ceramic flooring and back-painted glass panel siding.

Interlocking Bench

Furniture Design

This is a furniture studio project from my Graduate school at University of Oregon. The main concept of this design is utilizing 1 sheet of 4'x 8' plywood to construct majority of the bench. The w supporting arches are laminated using 1/8" door skin. The bench is designed for easy assemble and disassemble using only 12 bolts to secure the arches and the side planks. The rest of the bench is made of interlocking plywood planks. after disamble the largest piece is no more than 8' x 72" for easy transport and storage.
